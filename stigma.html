What Impacts Student's Health?

One in five canadians experience a mental health issue each year.

So What?

  • Many Canadians have reported they have faced embarrassment or discrimination because of their mental health diagnosis.
  • Stereotypes about people with mental illness are usually based on ignorance, misunderstanding, or misinformation.

Myth 3
People experiencing a mental illness are violent. People with a mental illness cannot function in society. People with mental illness should just snap out of it.
Fact:
There tends to be a public fear of potential violence and desire for limited social interaction usually associated with the behavior of someone suffering from mental illness. 		Fact:
A large number of people with mental illnesses or who experience mental health distress are highly functional contributors to society.		 Fact:
A mental illness is not a choice, and it is not easy to snap out of it. But with treatment and support, most people can regain their health.
